About

Hello, my name is Waridh Wongwandanee, I am also known as Bach. I am a Computer Engineering student at the University of Alberta. My specialization is in Nanoscale Systems Design, but my skillset is currently software dominant. I am fluent in C++, Matlab, Python, HTML, and CSS. I have some experiences using VHDL, and THUMB2 assembly.

In my first work term, I was employed by the Center for Smart Transportation (CST) as a research assistant. My responsibilities range from the maintenance of legacy code and documentation to the creation of new programs based on the needs of other researchers in the group.

So far the topics of my projects are computer vision related, from LiDAR point clouds to object detection and tracking. Further details on these projects can be found in the gallery tab of this website.
